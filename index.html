Huehuetenango sits at the northern tip of Guatemala’s Western Highlands, cut off from Pacific moisture by the Cuchumatanes mountain range. That isolation creates a dry microclimate where coffee develops slowly at extreme altitude — and slowly is exactly what builds this cup’s complexity. Stone fruit and florals emerge from beans that were never rushed.

Grown without chemicals on shade-covered hillsides in Chiapas, this coffee is as much an ethical position as it is a cup. The clean caramel sweetness and soft citrus finish are the direct result of healthy soil and unhurried farming.

God Within Every Cup.
Faith In Every Roast.

enTheos is not a product. It is a posture.

Every batch we roast is offered in gratitude — to the farmers who tend the land, to the craft that demands precision, and to God Almighty, the source of all good things. Our Philosophy

Artifacts.
Not
Accidents.

Every roast is the deliberate output
of structure, process, and precision.

Artifacts Coffee draws its name from a background in information systems and UX design, where “artefacts” represent the tangible outputs of research, analysis, and deliberate process.

In coffee, the same principle applies. Every roasted batch is the result of sourcing decisions, supplier relationships, roast development, and controlled execution. The final product is not accidental — it is the outcome of structure and precision.

Artifacts Coffee treats each roast as the final artefact of that process: clear in origin, intentional in development, and consistent in delivery.
