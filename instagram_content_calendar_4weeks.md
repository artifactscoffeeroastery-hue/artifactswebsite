**Format:** Carousel — infographic style (use brand yellow/dark) — visual explanation of washed process steps

**Caption:**
```
All three of our origins are washed. Here's why that matters.

The "process" refers to how the coffee cherry is prepared after harvest. With a washed (or fully washed) process, the fruit is completely removed before drying — so what you taste in the cup is pure bean, pure terroir, nothing else.

No fruit fermentation masking the profile. No added sweetness from the pulp.

Just the origin, unfiltered.

That's why our Guatemala shows you florals. Why Ki Saya's citrus is clean, not jammy. Why Rajuanse's chocolate is deep and even.

Washed = clarity. And clarity is the point.
```

# BRAND VOICE GUIDE

**Tone:** Direct, confident, unhurried. No fluff, no hype. Speaks like someone who knows exactly what they're doing and doesn't need to shout about it.

**What we never say:** "amazing," "game-changing," "crafted with love," "artisanal." We show, we don't perform.

**What we always do:** Earn the claim before we make it. Tell the origin story before we sell the bag. Give people a reason to care before we ask them to buy.

**Caption structure:**
1. Hook line — one sentence, sharp
2. The story or education (2–4 short paragraphs)
3. Tasting notes (bullet format only for products)
4. Price / CTA
5. Website or contact

**Image direction:**
- Natural light always
- White or stone/concrete surfaces
- Bags in frame — never just the cup
- Latte art for lifestyle, landscape for origin
- Yellow info cards as product slides (matches brand identity)
